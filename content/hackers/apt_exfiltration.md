### 🕵️ Advanced Persistent Threat (A.P.T.) Data Exfiltration (Confidentiality)
A long-term, highly sophisticated operation focused purely on remaining undetected while continuously siphoning massive amounts of sensitive data.

* **Deep Entrenchment:** The hackers establish multiple, redundant backdoors across the network, ensuring that if one access point is discovered by IT, they do not lose their foothold.
* **Egress Tunneling:** The attackers set up an encrypted tunnel—often disguising the stolen data as normal DNS requests or HTTPS web traffic—to bypass egress firewalls and Data Loss Prevention (DLP) monitors.
* **Slow Drip Extraction:** To avoid triggering bandwidth usage alarms, the data is compressed into small archives and extracted slowly, usually during off-hours or weekends over a period of several months.
* **High-Value Targeting:** The focus is purely on monetizable or strategically valuable data: unhashed passwords, PCI-DSS credit card databases, unreleased intellectual property, and executive communications.
