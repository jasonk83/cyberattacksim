### 💀 Double Extortion Ransomware (Availability & Confidentiality)
A catastrophic attack where threat actors first exfiltrate sensitive corporate data before deploying encryption malware, creating two distinct points of leverage against the target.

* **Silent Exfiltration:** Before triggering the alarms, hackers use automated scripts to quietly siphon terabytes of proprietary source code, customer PII, and financial records to an off-shore, encrypted server.
* **Mass Encryption:** Once the data is secured, the ransomware payload is executed network-wide, utilizing asymmetric encryption (like AES-256) to render operational databases and backups completely unusable.
* **The Dual Ultimatum:** The ransom note demands payment not just for the decryption key, but also for the promise *not* to leak the stolen data on the dark web.
* **Bypassing Backups:** This attack renders traditional IT backup strategies moot; even if the company can restore their systems from a clean offline backup, they are still forced to pay to prevent a massive public data breach.
