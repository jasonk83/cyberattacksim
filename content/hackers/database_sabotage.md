### 🔀 Database Integrity Sabotage (Integrity)
A highly destructive, stealthy attack where hackers subtly alter, scramble, or corrupt core business data over time, destroying the organization's ability to trust its own operational systems.

* **Stealth Modification:** Rather than locking data (which triggers immediate alarms), hackers write scripts that randomly swap shipping addresses, alter inventory counts by 5%, or change decimal placements on financial ledgers.
* **Delayed Detection:** Because the systems remain online and functional, the business continues to operate for days or weeks, completely unaware that they are processing corrupted transactions.
* **Backup Contamination:** By the time the sabotage is detected (usually via customer complaints of wrong shipments or massive accounting errors), the corrupted data has already been written into the company's automated backups.
* **The Rebuild Nightmare:** IT cannot simply "restore" the system. They must undergo a painstaking forensic audit, comparing weeks of transaction logs manually to identify which records are real and which were manipulated.
